The Tourist Visa Trap
Using a tourist visa to complete clinical rotations is illegal in several key countries. In India, the NMC requirement mandates a student visa for all clinical floors â€” tourists found on wards face deportation. South Africa requires HPCSA registration, which functions as a temporary work license. The UK allows electives on a Standard Visitor visa but requires a host university letter confirming it's a permitted medical elective activity.

South Africa: HPCSA Registration
The Health Professions Council of South Africa (HPCSA) registration is mandatory for all clinical rotations in South Africa. This process can take several months and is usually facilitated by the host university. At UCT/Groote Schuur, the ZAR 7,500 fee is inclusive of HPCSA registration. Plan for a 3â€“6 month lead time minimum. Do not book flights before confirmation.
