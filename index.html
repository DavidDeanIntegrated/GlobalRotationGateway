Before You Apply

Administrative & Regulatory Requirements

The most common sources of delay and failure for medical electives ‚Äî know these before you start applying.

üõÇ
The Tourist Visa Trap
Using a tourist visa to complete clinical rotations is illegal in several key countries covered on this site. In India, a student visa is mandatory for all clinical floors ‚Äî tourists found on wards face deportation. South Africa requires HPCSA registration, which functions as a temporary clinical license. Thailand (Ramathibodi) requires a Thai Non-Immigrant Education (ED) Visa from a Royal Thai Embassy before travel ‚Äî entering on a tourist visa is explicitly not legally permitted. Brazil requires a student visa for all elective routes. The UK allows electives on a Standard Visitor visa but requires a host university letter confirming the activity as a permitted medical elective.
üè•
South Africa: HPCSA Registration
The Health Professions Council of South Africa (HPCSA) registration is mandatory for all clinical rotations in South Africa. This process can take several months and is usually facilitated by the host university. At UCT/Groote Schuur, the ZAR 7,500 fee is inclusive of HPCSA registration. Plan for a 3‚Äì6 month lead time minimum. Do not book flights before confirmation.
üáÆüá≥
India: NMC + Ministry Clearance
Most Indian rotations require clearance from both the National Medical Commission (NMC) and the Indian Ministry of Health (plus the Ministry of External Affairs at some institutions). At CMC Vellore, this multi-agency process takes 3‚Äì4 months and requested dates cannot be changed once clearance is granted. Important exception: AIIMS New Delhi explicitly exempts foreign undergraduate medical students from NMC clearance ‚Äî a key logistical advantage. Regardless of route, a student visa is mandatory at both institutions ‚Äî tourist visas for clinical work are strictly illegal in India.
üìã
VSLO: The U.S. Gatekeeper
The Visiting Student Learning Opportunities (VSLO) program managed by AAMC is the primary gateway to most U.S. clinical electives. Participation is generally restricted to students from the "Global Network" ‚Äî a specific list of international medical schools that have paid to join the AAMC system. Ensure your home institution is a VSLO member before applying. Visit vslo.aamc.org ‚Üó
üõ°Ô∏è
Medical Malpractice Insurance
Most VSLO institutions require $1M per occurrence / $3M aggregate coverage. European institutions often require personal liability insurance separately. UK students covered worldwide (excluding USA) by MDU or MPS for electives. Argentina requires proof of malpractice insurance alongside your Dean's letter. Never assume your home school's policy covers international clinical work ‚Äî verify explicitly.
üí∞
Funding Sources
Erasmus+: EU students rotating within the EU (e.g., Graz ‚Üí Berlin). CFHI offers diversity scholarships for their programs. Yale, UMN and other US institutions have internal Global Health travel grants for their own students. Many medical schools have a global health officer who knows of institutional grants ‚Äî always ask. Search AAMC Global Health ‚Üó for additional resources.
Expert Guidance

How to Choose the Right Program

Based on your learning objective ‚Äî not just your destination bucket list.

01
For Trauma Skills ‚Üí South Africa
Apply to Chris Hani Baragwanath (via Wits) or Kenyatta National Hospital (Kenya). Baragwanath's penetrating trauma volume is unmatched globally; students consistently report greater hands-on involvement than any Western training environment. Start the HPCSA process 6 months early.
02
For Tropical Medicine ‚Üí India or Thailand

Key Terms Glossary

VSLO
Visiting Student Learning Opportunities ‚Äî AAMC's centralized platform for U.S. and international student exchange. Required for most U.S. electives. Your home institution must be a paid VSLO Global Network member.

The German term for a clinical elective rotation. Used at Charit√© Berlin and other German medical institutions. Typically requires B2/C1 German for patient contact.

Health Professions Council of South Africa. Registration is mandatory before any clinical activity in South Africa. Usually facilitated by host university. Process takes 2‚Äì6 months.
